Control & Metrics Corner

How Kaiser measures success beyond traditional healthcare metrics

Performance Measurement Framework

Housing Outcomes

  • • Number of patients housed
  • • Housing stability duration
  • • Time from identification to placement

Health Outcomes

  • • Reduction in emergency department visits
  • • Improvement in chronic disease management
  • • Mental health service utilization

Economic Outcomes

  • • Healthcare cost savings
  • • Reduction in high-cost services
  • • Return on investment calculations

System Outcomes

  • • Staff engagement metrics
  • • Community partner satisfaction
  • • Program scalability assessments

Key Strategic Insights

System Interdependence

The initiative demonstrates how organizational systems and control metrics must evolve together—Kaiser's data infrastructure enabled new measurement capabilities, while their metrics reinforced the importance of the system investments.

Vertical Integration

By addressing housing (typically outside healthcare's scope), Kaiser achieved deeper vertical integration of social determinants into care delivery, creating a competitive advantage in population health management.

Metric Evolution

The program represents a shift from process metrics (how many patients screened) to outcome metrics (how many housed with improved health), demonstrating strategic maturity.

Scalability Tension

While successful, the program faces scalability challenges due to regional housing market variations—highlighting the tension between standardized systems and localized implementation.
